## Goal

Use the labeled `iv_anomalies` dataset (15,886 backfill rows + ongoing live
rows) to characterize which detector signals and gate combinations actually
predict tradeable price movement, per ticker and per market regime. Output:
a per-ticker "signal quality" report that informs threshold tuning and
trader confidence.

## Why now

After 2026-04-25 we have:

- **15,886 backfill alerts** across 13 tickers and 10 days, generated by
  the production `detectAnomalies()` function on real CSV data
- **740,120 strike_iv_snapshots rows** â€” full IV time series per strike
  (chart-driving fields)
- **Per-ticker signal density variance** (TSLA 1015 / META 286 / SMH 9)
  that hints at meaningful structural differences in informed-flow
  characteristics
- **Three exit-signal types** now clearly distinguished:
  `iv_regression`, `ask_mid_compression`, `bid_side_surge`

The dataset is thin (10 days) but structurally rich enough to do a first-pass
characterization that informs gate tuning before the next 2-4 weeks of live
data thicken the population.

## Core questions

1. **Per-ticker peak-return distribution.** What does the empirical
   distribution of intraday peak return look like, conditioned on the gate
   that fired? Does TSLA's 1015 alerts produce 5Ă— more "actionable"
   signals than META's 286, or just 5Ă— the noise?

2. **Signal-type stratification.** Of alerts that fired with `skew_delta`
   only vs `skew_delta + z_score` vs `skew_delta + z_score + ask_mid_div` â€”
   which combination has the highest hit rate (peak return â‰Ą +30%)? The
   spec hypothesizes more flag reasons = stronger signal; needs validation.

3. **Time-of-day effect.** Does the alert's CT-time bucket affect
   outcomes? Specifically: 8:30-9:30 (open auction), 9:30-12:00
   (institutional window), 12:00-15:00 (afternoon)? User's trading
   schedule explicitly avoids 14:30-15:00 which is consistent with the
   prior assumption that closing-hour alerts are reactive.

4. **Vol/OI threshold tuning.** The current 5Ă— gate is empirical â€”
   confirm by sweeping vol/OI ranges (5-10Ă—, 10-50Ă—, 50-200Ă—, 200Ă—+) and
   measuring outcome means + variances per bucket.

5. **OTM-distance effect.** For cash-index whales (now captured at Â±12%),
   does outcome quality monotonically increase with OTM distance, or is
   there a sweet spot?

6. **Side-skew vs side-dominant.** The 0.65 side-skew threshold gate is
   empirical. Does signal quality increase as side_skew approaches 1.0,
   or does it plateau? Most production rows show side_skew in the
   0.7-0.9 range â€” is that a measurement-driven distribution or
   threshold-driven?

7. **flow_phase outcome differentiation.** Now that backfill rows have
   meaningful early/mid/reactive labels (Phase 3 of 2026-04-25 work),
   does early consistently outperform mid which consistently outperforms
   reactive? If not, the classifier needs work.

## Approach

### Stage 1 â€” Data extraction (1h)

Build `ml/extract-iv-anomalies-features.py` that:

1. Pulls all `iv_anomalies` backfill rows from Neon
2. For each anomaly, computes outcome features by joining
   `strike_iv_snapshots` for the same `(ticker, strike, side, expiry)`:
   - `peak_iv_after`, `min_iv_after` (rolling forward from `ts`)
   - `peak_premium_after` derived from `mid_price` post-anomaly
   - `peak_premium_pct` = (peak / entry) - 1
   - `time_to_peak_min`
   - `eod_pnl_pct` = (close / entry) - 1
3. Adds derived features:
   - `otm_distance_pct` = abs(strike - spot) / spot
   - `signal_count` = len(flag_reasons) â€” 1 (subtract 'backfill' tag)
   - `ct_minute_of_day` = ts â†’ CT minute
4. Outputs `ml/data/iv-anomaly-outcomes.parquet`

### Stage 2 â€” EDA (~1h)

Build `ml/eda-iv-anomalies.py` matching the existing ml/ EDA pattern. Plots:

- Per-ticker peak_pct CDF (one line per ticker on one chart)
- Peak_pct by signal_count (1 vs 2 vs 3 reasons)
- Peak_pct by flow_phase (early vs mid vs reactive)
- Peak_pct by ct_hour bucket (heatmap)
- Vol/OI ratio bucket â†’ peak_pct boxplot
- OTM distance â†’ peak_pct scatter (colored by ticker tier)
- side_skew distribution (histogram per ticker)

Output: `ml/plots/iv-anomaly-eda-2026-04-25/*.png` (committed per
`feedback_keep_ml_plots_in_git.md`).

### Stage 3 â€” Stratified analysis + threshold tuning (~1h)

Output: `ml/findings/iv-anomaly-stratified-2026-04-25.json` + a

## Constraints

- **Sample size** â€” 10 days Ă— 13 tickers = thin. Per-ticker subsets get
  small fast. Treat results as directional, not statistically conclusive.
- **flow_phase coverage limits** â€” Only 56% of backfill rows have VIX
  data (4/17 onwards). Stratifying by flow_phase reduces effective n on
  early-day rows.
- **Cross-asset context absent** â€” backfill `context_snapshot` only has
  `vix_level` + `vix_delta_15m`. Cannot study e.g. "alerts during high
  VIX vs low VIX" with much depth.
- **Live rows arriving daily** â€” re-run weekly to get a thicker
  population.

## Out of scope

- Predictive ML model training (this is exploratory, not predictive).
  After 4-6 weeks of live data, a Phase 5 spec can revisit modeling.
- Cross-asset feature engineering (separate spec â€” would require
  enriching `context_snapshot` retroactively from `futures_bars` etc.)
- Backtest of "trade every alert" strategies â€” outcome characterization
  first, strategy design separately.
